Kaley Brauer
Astrophysics Ph.D. Student, Massachusetts Institute of Technology

Computational Astrophysicist

I study galaxy formation and the astrophysical origins of heavy elements. My specialty is the smallest, earliest galaxies that merged into the Milky Way throughout cosmic time. My work uses high performance computing and statistical methods.

Currently, I am working towards my Physics Ph.D. in MIT's Physics Department & Kavli Institute for Astrophysics and Space Research. Previously, I obtained a B.Sc. in Physics from Brown University.

When I am not doing research, I enjoy science outreach, dancing, art, hiking, and riding bikes.
